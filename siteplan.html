Site Name

Temple Discovery Hub

Proposed Domain: templediscoveryhub.org

Why This Name?

The name "Temple Discovery Hub" was chosen for several important reasons:

  • Clarity: The word "Temple" immediately communicates the site's focus on temples of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
  • Discovery: This word invites exploration and learning, appealing to both members seeking to learn more about temples and visitors curious about these sacred structures
  • Hub: Positions the site as a central resource and gathering place for temple information, creating a sense of community
  • Universal Appeal: The name is welcoming and educational, not overly formal, making it accessible to various audiences
  • SEO Friendly: Contains key search terms people would use when looking for temple information online

Site Purpose

Temple Discovery Hub serves as a comprehensive digital resource for exploring temples of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints worldwide.

Primary Goals:

  • Education: Provide detailed information about temples including their history, architecture, dedication dates, and locations
  • Visual Experience: Showcase beautiful imagery of temples through an organized, filterable gallery
  • Accessibility: Make temple information easily accessible through intuitive filtering by size, age, and location
  • Inspiration: Inspire visitors through the beauty and significance of these sacred buildings
  • Community Engagement: Create a platform where visitors can learn about temple open houses, dedications, and visiting opportunities

Services Provided:

  • Interactive temple gallery with multiple filter options (by age, size, location)
  • Detailed temple profiles including square footage, dedication dates, and locations
  • Responsive design for optimal viewing on all devices
  • Search functionality to quickly find specific temples
  • Educational resources about temple significance and purpose

Target Audience Scenarios

These scenarios represent common questions from our target audience, helping drive the content and functionality of the website:

Scenario 1: The Curious Researcher

"I'm writing a research paper on religious architecture. Which are the largest temples, and when were they built?"

How the site helps: The "Large" filter allows users to quickly identify temples over 90,000 square feet. Each temple card displays the dedication date and total area, making it easy to gather this research data efficiently.

Scenario 2: The History Enthusiast

"I'm interested in historic temples. Which temples were built in the 1800s?"

How the site helps: The "Old" filter specifically shows temples dedicated before 1900, allowing history enthusiasts to explore the earliest temples with their unique architectural styles and historical significance.

Scenario 3: The Travel Planner

"I'm planning a trip to Japan. Is there a temple there I could visit?"

How the site helps: Users can browse all temples and search for specific locations. Each temple card displays the location, making it easy to identify temples in desired travel destinations.

Scenario 4: The New Member

"I recently joined the Church. What are the newest temples, and where are they located?"

How the site helps: The "New" filter shows temples built after 2000, perfect for members wanting to learn about recent temple construction and expansion. Location information helps them find temples near their area.

Scenario 5: The Mobile User

"I want to show my friend some beautiful temple photos while we're out. Can I do this on my phone?"

How the site helps: The fully responsive design ensures beautiful display on all devices, with a mobile-optimized menu and touch-friendly interface for easy browsing on smartphones.

Color Schema

The color palette reflects spirituality, trust, and elegance associated with temples:

Primary Color: Royal Blue

Hex: #2c5282

RGB: rgb(44, 82, 130)

Usage:

  • Header background (gradient with lighter blue)
  • Primary headings (H1, H2)
  • Navigation active states
  • Call-to-action buttons
  • Footer background

Why chosen: Blue represents trust, wisdom, and spirituality - perfect for a religious site. This deeper shade conveys authority and reverence.

Secondary Color: Sky Blue

Hex: #3182ce

RGB: rgb(49, 130, 206)

Usage:

  • Gradient partner with royal blue in headers
  • Hover states on navigation
  • Links and interactive elements
  • Accent borders
  • Filter status backgrounds

Why chosen: A brighter blue that complements the primary color while adding visual interest and energy to the design.

Accent Color: Gold

Hex: #ffd700

RGB: rgb(255, 215, 0)

Usage:

  • Active navigation indicators
  • Hover highlights
  • Important call-out text
  • Icons and decorative elements

Why chosen: Gold symbolizes divinity, quality, and importance. It provides strong contrast against blue and draws attention to key interactive elements.

Base Color: White

Hex: #ffffff

RGB: rgb(255, 255, 255)

Usage:

  • Temple card backgrounds
  • Main content areas
  • Text on dark backgrounds
  • Clean, spacious feeling

Why chosen: White represents purity and cleanliness, essential qualities associated with temples. Provides excellent readability.

Background Color: Off-White

Hex: #f8f9fa

RGB: rgb(248, 249, 250)

Usage:

  • Page background
  • Subtle section dividers
  • Reduces eye strain

Why chosen: Softer than pure white, reducing screen glare while maintaining cleanliness.

Text Color: Charcoal

Hex: #333333

RGB: rgb(51, 51, 51)

Usage:

  • Body text
  • Descriptions
  • Temple information details

Why chosen: Softer than pure black (#000000), reducing eye strain while maintaining excellent readability.

H1: Main Page Title

H2: Section Heading

H3: Subsection or Card Title

Body Text: Regular paragraph content uses Lato at 16px with 1.6 line-height for comfortable reading. This ensures accessibility and reduces eye strain during extended browsing sessions.

Bold Body Text: Used for emphasis and labels

Caption Text: Smaller text for metadata and supplementary information (14px)

Accessibility Considerations

  • Font sizes are at least 16px for body text
  • Line height is 1.6 for improved readability
  • Adequate spacing between elements
  • High contrast ratios between text and background
  • Fonts remain legible when scaled up to 200%
